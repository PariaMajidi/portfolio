This case study is about Claim History portion

What is Claim History?

Claim history is where users can find and track their submitted claims

The goal for 2024?

How might we make it easier for members to find their submitted claims and track its progress ?

How Claim History redesign started?

The business made a new contract with a new client and promised to give a real time decision on the submitted claims. In this way, users can see how much they are going to be paid, if any, right after they submit their claims. They can also visit claim history and find out how much they will be paid even if their claim has not processed yet.

The business wanted to add a new feature to the claim history experience. But is the current experience effective, and will this addition improve it or complicate it?

Addressing usability issues or pursuing feature growth?

  • The mobile team conducted two usability studies for Claim History and found areas that confuse users.
  • The question was, whether to address usability issues first OR to implement real time response for claim history and then improve UX.
  • We had multiple meetings with the product and technical teams to understand the cost of each.

Design Discovery and empathizing:

Old design

Mobile team usability study summary:

The method: Unmoderated UZ advanced UX study, Participants: 8, Audience: General population, Age: 25-55, Standard screener with users who have health benefits product, Launched: September 2, 2022

Summary of critical usability issues: (Defining)

Usability issue1:

Users unable to understand the difference between recent online and processed claims and why there is duplications.

“To be honest I’m a little confused about the difference between Processed claims and Recent online claims.”

“So, I found myself checking on both the tabs because I wasn’t sure which category it fell into” 

Usability issue2:

Showing Pending claims under processed claims confused users as their initial idea of the processed claims tab was only claims that are processed and paid would be there.

“So that’s interesting…I thought everything processed would be whatever that’s been paid, but I see that there is one still pending, I’m wondering wouldn’t that fall under recent online claims…”

Research Recommendations

  • Consider merging the processed and recent online claims pages because the users cannot understand the difference.
  • If keeping two tabs is required, they recommended the following:
    • Clarify the titles of the tabs, I.e., Users perceive processed claims as anything that has been processed and paid, but 'Under review' claims also show up in this tab.
    • Have the Processed claims tab house all claims that have been processed and paid out. Have the Recent online claims tab house all claims that have been submitted but are not yet processed.
    • Avoid overlap of showing the same claim on each tab.

The claim history page structure for categorizing claims based on their processing status didn’t align with user’s expectations.

We decided to ideate different designs and see the amount of effort and then decide which design we should go forward to meet the deadline, satisfy the business and the customer and being user friendly

Introduction

In the context of the UX Evaluation course at HEC Montreal, the student consultants conducted a usability evaluation and provided a set of recommendations to improve the user experience (UX) of a newly updated page of the website called rewards webpage. The project is for an international crowdfunding platform where the majority of projects get funded. Their mission is to support creators throughout this process by helping them make their ideas a reality, co-create with their communities, spread the word about their projects and make them grow. What is crowdfunding? It’s the act of raising money from a large number of people who each contribute a relatively small amount and will get (optionally) a reward in exchange. Essentially, rewards are a sign of gratitude to donors. As part of creating a project on the website, creators use the rewards web page to define what people will receive in exchange for their contribution. Except for vouchers, creators can create a variety of rewards. They must also define how rewards will be delivered. This is called delivery.

Evaluation Scope

Goals and Objectives

1. Evaluate if the UI correctly onboards new users to define and create their rewards

2. Evaluate if the distinction between rewards and delivery is understood by the user

3. Evaluate if the user will create its rewards first or its delivery settings, and why?

Test Procedure

Metrics

Usefulness, Efficiency, Effectiveness and Satisfaction

Usefulness is defined as the “degree to which a product enables a user to achieve his or her goals”. It was measured through the web pages’ perceived ease of use by participants.

efficiency was measured as the average time to perform a task and assessed based on issues observed during performance of the tasks.

effectiveness was measured as the percentage of tasks successfully completed.

Satisfaction was measured by the "Think Out Loud" comments shared during task scenarios and the post-study questionnaire.
